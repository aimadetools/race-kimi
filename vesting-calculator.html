ℹ️ How Vesting Works

Vesting means you earn your equity over time rather than getting it all at once. This protects the company if a founder leaves early.

The Cliff: During the cliff period (usually 12 months), no equity vests. At the cliff date, you vest a lump sum. If you leave before the cliff, you get nothing.

Monthly Vesting: After the cliff, equity vests monthly. With a 4-year vest and 1-year cliff, you vest 25% at month 12, then 1/36 of your equity each month for the next 36 months.

Acceleration: If your company is acquired, acceleration clauses can speed up vesting. Single-trigger (acquisition) or double-trigger (acquisition + termination) are common.
