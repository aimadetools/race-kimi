Key Differences

🎯 Focus vs. All-in-One

Changelog.page does one thing extremely well: changelogs. Canny is a full feedback platform with roadmaps, voting, and AI features. If you just need to share updates, you're paying for complexity you don't need.

💰 Predictable Pricing

Canny charges per "tracked user" — anyone who votes, comments, or views your board. With 1,000 tracked users, Canny Pro costs ~$661/mo. Changelog.page is flat $15/mo regardless of traffic.

⚡ Speed & Performance

Changelog.page generates static HTML files that load in under 100ms. Canny is a dynamic platform with ~500ms load times. For SEO and user experience, static wins.

🔒 Data Ownership

Your changelog lives in your GitHub repository. No vendor lock-in, no export headaches. With Canny, your data lives on their servers.

🛠️ Developer Workflow

Write changelogs in Markdown, commit to GitHub, auto-deploy. Developers love it. Canny requires using their web interface or complex API integrations.

📈 SEO Control

Static changelogs are fully indexable by search engines. You control the HTML, meta tags, and structure. Canny's dynamic content has limited SEO visibility.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Canny's "tracked user" pricing? +
Canny charges based on "tracked users" — anyone who creates a post, votes, or comments on your board. Even your own team members count if they interact with feedback. With 1,000 tracked users, Canny Pro jumps from $79/mo to ~$661/mo. Most growing SaaS companies hit this quickly.
Does Changelog.page have feedback boards like Canny? +
No — and that's intentional. Changelog.page is focused purely on changelogs. If you need feedback boards and roadmaps, Canny is the better choice. But if you just need to share product updates, you're paying 5x more with Canny for features you won't use.
Can I migrate from Canny to Changelog.page? +
Yes! You can export your changelog entries from Canny and convert them to Markdown files. Our team can help with the migration — just email us at support@changelog.page. Most migrations take under an hour.
Which tool is better for SEO? +
Changelog.page wins for SEO. Static HTML pages load faster (<100ms), are fully indexable by search engines, and give you complete control over meta tags and structure. Canny's dynamic content has limited SEO benefits.
When should I choose Canny over Changelog.page? +
Choose Canny if you need: (1) Feedback collection and voting, (2) Public roadmaps, (3) User segmentation by revenue/MRR, or (4) CRM integrations (Salesforce, HubSpot). Choose Changelog.page if you just need a beautiful, fast, affordable changelog.
Is there a free plan? +
Canny offers a free plan limited to 25 tracked users with no custom domain. Changelog.page offers a 14-day free trial of our Pro plan. Our focus is on sustainable pricing for serious products, not freemium limits that force upgrades.
