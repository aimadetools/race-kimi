The Co-Founder Equity Checklist

A comprehensive guide to splitting equity fairly, protecting your startup, and avoiding founder disputes. Complete all critical items before you write your first line of code together.

⏱️ 30 minutes to complete
📄 Print or use digitally
🎯 22 action items
1

Before the Conversation

Prepare yourself and gather the necessary information

  • Research market rates for each founder's role High
    Check AngelList, Glassdoor, or industry reports for salary benchmarks in your market
  • Calculate each founder's time commitment Critical
    Hours per week × weeks committed = total time investment
  • Document any cash investments Critical
    Incorporation costs, software licenses, equipment, legal fees
  • List IP and prior work contributions High
    Code, designs, patents, domain names, customer relationships brought to the table
  • Assess experience level multipliers Medium
    First-time founder (1.0x), Some startup experience (1.2x), Serial founder/expert (1.5x)
💡 Pro Tip: Use our free equity calculator to get a data-driven starting point for your conversation. It factors in all the above inputs and gives you a suggested split.
2

The Equity Conversation

Have the crucial discussion with your co-founder

  • Schedule a dedicated, uninterrupted meeting Critical
    This is not a conversation to have over Slack. Book 2 hours with no distractions.
  • Share your preparation work transparently Critical
    Both founders should share their time, cash, and IP calculations openly
  • Discuss roles and responsibilities Critical
    Who is CEO? Who handles product, engineering, sales, operations? Write it down.
  • Agree on a decision-making framework High
    What happens when you disagree? Simple majority? CEO tie-breaker? Veto rights?
  • Discuss vesting terms upfront Critical
    4-year vesting with 1-year cliff is standard. Both founders should vest equally.
⚠️ Warning: If you can't have this conversation without tension, that's a red flag. Co-founder disputes are the #1 cause of startup failure. Address issues now before they fester.
3

Making It Legal

Document everything with proper agreements

  • Sign a Co-Founder Agreement Critical
    Equity split, roles, vesting schedule, decision-making, and dispute resolution
  • Complete IP Assignment Agreements Critical
    Transfer all pre-existing IP and future work to the company. Investors will require this.
  • File 83(b) Election within 30 days Critical
    Mail to IRS within 30 days of receiving equity or face massive tax penalties
  • Sign Confidentiality Agreements High
    Protect trade secrets, customer lists, and proprietary information
  • Define termination and departure terms Critical
    What happens if someone leaves? Good leaver vs bad leaver provisions
  • Consider non-compete clauses Medium
    Prevent departed founders from starting competing businesses (check local enforceability)
4

Ongoing Maintenance

Keep your agreement current as things change

  • Schedule quarterly equity check-ins High
    Review if the split still feels fair given actual contributions vs. projections
  • Document any significant contribution changes High
    If someone increases/decreases time commitment, write it down and both sign
  • Update agreements when raising funding Critical
    Investors will require clean agreements. Update before due diligence, not during.
  • Maintain a cap table High
    Track all equity, options, and ownership changes from day one
  • Store documents securely with backups High
    Originals in safe deposit box, scanned copies in cloud storage, both founders have access
💡 Pro Tip: Set calendar reminders for your quarterly check-ins now. It's easy to forget once you're in the weeds building.
