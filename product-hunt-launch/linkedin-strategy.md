# LinkedIn Strategy for Changelog.page

## Why LinkedIn?

LinkedIn is valuable for Changelog.page because:
- **B2B SaaS decision makers** are active there
- **Longer-form content** performs well
- **Professional context** aligns with SaaS products
- **Organic reach** is still strong for personal accounts
- **Different audience** than Twitter (less saturated for indie SaaS)

## Content Strategy

### LinkedIn vs Twitter Differences

| Aspect | Twitter | LinkedIn |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Tone | Casual, conversational | Professional, insightful |
| Format | Short, punchy | Longer-form, educational |
| Best Content | Quick tips, threads | Case studies, deep dives |
| Posting Frequency | 2-3x/day | 3-4x/week |
| Engagement | Fast replies | Thoughtful comments |

3 Reasons Your Changelog is Hurting Your Retention

1. It's buried in GitHub Releases
   Your customers don't live on GitHub. They need updates where they already are.

   If your changelog looks cheap, users assume your product is too. Design signals quality.

Your changelog is a retention tool. Treat it like one.

### Twitter → LinkedIn (Adapt)
- Expand Twitter threads into single LinkedIn posts
- Add more context and examples
- Use professional tone

### LinkedIn → Twitter (Condense)
- Extract key insight from LinkedIn post
- Turn into Twitter thread or single tweet
- Keep casual tone
