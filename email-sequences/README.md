# Email Sequences for Changelog.page

Complete email marketing sequences for user engagement, onboarding, and conversion.

---

## Sequences Overview

| Sequence | Purpose | Trigger | Emails |
|----------|---------|---------|--------|
| **Welcome** | Confirm waitlist signup, set expectations | Form submission | 1 |
| **Onboarding** | Guide new users to first changelog | Account creation | 5 |
| **Pro Upgrade** | Convert free users to Pro plan | 7 days post-setup or feature usage | 4 |
| **Newsletter** | Weekly value + product updates | Weekly (Tuesdays) | Ongoing |

---

## Files

- `welcome-email.md` — Waitlist confirmation & expectations
- `onboarding-sequence.md` — 5-email new user journey
- `pro-upgrade-sequence.md` — 4-email conversion sequence
- `newsletter-template.md` — Reusable weekly newsletter format

---

## Sending Infrastructure

### Current (Waitlist Phase)
- **Service**: FormSubmit.co (free)
- **Method**: Form submission sends auto-reply
- **Limitation**: No automated sequences

### Future (Post-Launch)
Recommended options:
1. **Buttondown** ($9/mo) — Simple, markdown-friendly
2. **ConvertKit** (free up to 1,000) — Better automation
3. **Mailchimp** (free up to 500) — Industry standard
4. **Loops** (free tier) — Modern, API-friendly

---

## Brand Voice Guidelines

- **Tone**: Helpful, friendly, knowledgeable but not preachy
- **Perspective**: Fellow builder, not corporate vendor
- **Length**: Scannable, 150-300 words per email
- **CTA**: One clear action per email
- **Sign-off**: Personal from the founder (when possible)

---

## Subject Line Patterns That Work

1. **Curiosity gap**: "The one thing most changelogs get wrong"
2. **Direct benefit**: "Your changelog is ready"
3. **Personal**: "[Name], quick question about your setup"
4. **Urgency (sparingly)**: "24 hours left: Pro early-bird pricing"
5. **How-to**: "How to get your first 100 changelog views"
