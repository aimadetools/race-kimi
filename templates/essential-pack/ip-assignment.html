3. DEFINITION OF INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

For purposes of this Agreement, "Intellectual Property" or "IP" includes, but is not limited to:

3.1 Technology IP

  • Software code, source code, object code, algorithms, and scripts
  • Databases, data structures, and data compilations
  • Technical documentation and specifications
  • APIs, SDKs, and integration tools
  • Cloud infrastructure configurations

3.2 Creative IP

  • Logos, brand marks, and graphic designs
  • User interface (UI) and user experience (UX) designs
  • Marketing materials, copy, and content
  • Photography, video, and multimedia content
  • Product names and taglines

3.3 Business IP

  • Business plans, financial models, and strategies
  • Customer lists, supplier lists, and contact databases
  • Pricing information and competitive intelligence
  • Partnership agreements and negotiations

3.4 Legal IP

  • Patent applications, patents, and invention disclosures
  • Trademark applications and registered trademarks
  • Copyright registrations and applications
  • Trade secrets and confidential know-how
  • Domain names and social media handles

4. ASSIGNMENT OF IP

4.1 Current Assignment

Assignor hereby assigns, transfers, and conveys to the Company all right, title, and interest in and to:

  • All Intellectual Property listed in Exhibit A attached hereto
  • All Intellectual Property created by Assignor prior to the Effective Date that relates to the Company's current or proposed business
  • All goodwill associated with such Intellectual Property

4.2 Future Assignment

Assignor hereby assigns to the Company all right, title, and interest in and to any Intellectual Property that Assignor may create, conceive, or develop:

  • During the period of Assignor's relationship with the Company
  • Using Company equipment, supplies, facilities, or resources
  • Relating to the Company's current or anticipated business
  • At the Company's request or on the Company's behalf
Important: This includes IP created during evenings and weekends, even if not explicitly requested by the Company, provided it relates to the Company's business.

4.3 Moral Rights

To the extent permitted by applicable law, Assignor hereby waives all moral rights, including rights of attribution and integrity, with respect to any Intellectual Property assigned hereunder.

  • Other: [SPECIFY]

6. ASSIGNOR REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES

Assignor represents and warrants that:

  1. Assignor is the sole and exclusive owner of all Intellectual Property being assigned
  2. The Intellectual Property is free from any liens, encumbrances, or security interests
  3. The Intellectual Property does not infringe upon any third-party rights
  4. Assignor has not previously assigned, licensed, or transferred any rights in the IP to any third party
  5. No third party has any claim to the Intellectual Property
  6. Assignor has the full right and authority to enter into this Agreement
  7. All employees, contractors, or collaborators who contributed to the IP have assigned their rights to Assignor (or will do so)

7. FURTHER ASSURANCE

Assignor agrees to:

  • Execute any additional documents necessary to perfect the Company's ownership
  • Cooperate in filing patent, trademark, or copyright applications
  • Provide testimony and evidence in any proceedings to enforce Company IP rights
  • Disclose all material information known about the Intellectual Property
  • Secure assignments from any subcontractors or collaborators

8. CONFIDENTIALITY

Assignor acknowledges that the Intellectual Property and related information constitute valuable trade secrets and confidential information of the Company.

Assignor agrees to maintain strict confidentiality and not disclose such information to any third parties without written consent.

9. TERMINATION OF RELATIONSHIP

This Agreement and the assignment of Intellectual Property hereunder shall survive:

  • The termination of Assignor's employment or consulting relationship with the Company
  • The dissolution or bankruptcy of the Company
  • Any merger or acquisition of the Company

The obligations in Sections 4, 6, 7, and 8 shall survive indefinitely.
