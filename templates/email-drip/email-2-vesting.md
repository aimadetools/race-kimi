Hi again,

Quick question: What happens if your co-founder decides to leave after 6 months?

Without vesting, they keep their full equity stake forever. Even if they stopped contributing. Even if you end up doing 10x more work.

That's not a partnership. That's a time bomb.

**Here's how vesting protects both of you:**

✓ **The Cliff (usually 12 months)**  
If someone leaves before the cliff, they get nothing. This ensures everyone's committed for the hard first year.

✓ **Gradual Vesting After Cliff**  
After month 12, equity vests monthly. If someone leaves at month 18, they keep 18/48 of their equity (37.5%), not 100%.

✓ **Fair to Everyone**  
The person who stays and builds the company keeps more. The person who leaves early keeps less. Fair.

**Real scenario:**
Two founders split 50/50. One quits at month 9 due to family issues. Without vesting: they keep 50% forever. With 4-year vesting + 12-month cliff: they keep 0%. The staying founder can use that equity to hire a replacement.
