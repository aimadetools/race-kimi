Common Questions

Is a 50/50 split ever a good idea?

Sometimes—but only when contributions, risk, and roles are truly equal. Most startups have asymmetry (one founder quits a job, another keeps theirs; one brings IP, the other brings cash). FairSplit's calculator helps you find the split that matches reality, not friendship.

Can't we just use a free template from the internet?

Free templates often miss critical clauses like 83(b) election guidance, double-trigger acceleration, and pre-existing IP disclosure. FairSplit's templates were built specifically for startup co-founders and cover the edge cases that sink deals.

When should we actually hire a lawyer?

Hire a lawyer when you have a complex cap table, multiple funding rounds, or non-standard terms. For most pre-seed and bootstrapped startups with 2–4 co-founders, FairSplit covers 90% of what you need at 3% of the cost.

What happens if we don't document our equity split?

Disputes become "he said, she said." IP ownership gets murky. Investors demand a clean cap table and may walk if agreements are missing. The cost of fixing it later is always higher than doing it right today.
